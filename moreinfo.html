Q) Why donate blood?

A decision to donate your blood can save a life, or even several if your blood is separated into its components like red cells, platelets and plasma – which can be used individually for patients with specific conditions.


Q) What are the benefits of donating blood

1. Reduce risk of heart attacks and liver ailment Donating blood regularly is beneficial to prevent and reduce heart attacks and liver ailment. The risk of heart and liver related problem is caused by the iron overload in the body. Donating blood helps in maintaining the iron level in the body and thus reduce those risk.

2. Lower the risk of cancer Cancer is the most feared and deadly disease. Blood donation helps in lowering the risk of cancer. By donating blood regularly the iron level in the blood is balanced and the risk of cancer-related to the liver, lungs, and intestine gets lower

3. New blood cells Once we donate blood, the body tries to restore the blood loss. This helps in the production of the new blood cells and maintain good health


Q) Why is blood needed?

There are many reasons patients need blood. A common misunderstanding about blood usage is that accident victims are the patients who use the most blood. Actually, people needing the most blood include those:

1.Being treated for cancer

2.Undergoing orthopedic surgeries

3.Undergoing cardiovascular surgeries

4.Being treated for inherited blood disorders


Q) What are different types of blood groups?

There are 8 possible blood groups. O-ve , O+ve , A-ve , A+ve , B-ve , B+ve , AB-ve and AB+ve

Facts about blood donation!!

1. Just 1 donation can save up to 3 lives

2. The average red blood cell transfusion is 3 pints (or 3 whole-blood donations)

3. More than 1 million people every year are diagnosed with cancer for the first time. Many of them will need blood—sometimes daily—during chemotherapy. More than 38,000 blood donations are needed every day

4. Type O-negative whole blood can be transfused to people with any blood type, but this type of blood is rare, and supplies of it are low

5. Type AB plasma can be transfused to patients with all other blood types, but it's also in short supply
